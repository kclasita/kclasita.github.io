A little more about me

I’m a user experience designer at Gaslight , a Cincinnati software development firm that transforms businesses with web-based technology. As the company’s first-ever designer, I started Gaslight’s design practice and created all the visuals for the brand, from the logo to a website and style guide.

On a daily basis, you’ll often find me working on client projects and trying to create designs that elevate the user experience without complicating it. I’m constantly trying to balance time, beauty and function during the process of designing and building an app.

I’m a graduate of the University of Cincinnati College of Design, Architecture, Art and Planning (DAAP). During my college years, I completed design co-ops at LPK and Jack Rouse Associates in Cincinnati, as well as Stoltze Design in Boston. I also spent several months as a co-op at Infinite Scale in Salt Lake City, working on environmental graphics for the Orange Bowl and Sundance Film Festival.

When I first joined Gaslight, I was completely new to HTML and CSS, but I picked up the ability to build my designs working side-by-side with developers. Now I even share my design and development experience at tech conferences and take on small freelance projects in my spare time.

I live in a Cincinnati neighborhood called Oakley with my dog, Ellie. My other loves include crafting, traveling and leather shoes.
